Ihnwhi Heo

PUBLISHED ON JUL 21, 2021

In retrospect, I have turned from a junior researcher who liked social psychology and was trained to find significant p-values into a methodologist who actively criticizes research misconducts and promotes the idea of Bayesian statistics. It was coincidental how I delved into the data-intensive aspect of psychology. When working as a research assistant at Utrecht University, the first role assigned to me was to code reported results for a meta-analysis. I was amazed by the idea of summarizing multiple results together.

As I continued learning techniques such as structural equation modeling, multilevel modeling, Bayesian analyses, I became more and more interested in applications of these methods to substantive psychology in general. I was highly motivated thanks to the potential of quantitative psychology to answer complex research questions. This worked well when I performed a multilevel mediation analysis for a project I worked on at Sungkyunkwan University.

I was sure I wanted to major in methodology and statistics, leading to a wonderful but challenging journey at Utrecht University. I engaged in methodological issues including redefining the p-value and had the joy of applying Bayesian statistics to social psychology. I was also involved in teaching where I led discussions to criticize erroneous practices in published research and taught advanced statistical concepts including mixed design, mediation, and moderation. I am currently writing a master’s thesis about Bayesian structural equation modeling under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Eric-Jan Wagenmakers, my Bayesian idol. Besides research and teaching, it is another delight to present my work and discuss new ideas with my fabulous classmates.
